## Why?

Most knowledge workers already use AI — but without structure. They copy-paste into ChatGPT, get decent results sometimes, and have no idea why it works or when it doesn't. There's no onboarding, no curriculum, no progression. AI Fluency closes that gap: a free, open learning path from first conversations to autonomous delegation. No coding required.

## Curriculum

| Level | Topic | What you'll learn |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **L1** | First Steps with AI | What AI actually is, tool landscape, first conversations, understanding limits, do's & don'ts |
| **L2** | Intentional Prompting | Prompt anatomy, techniques (few-shot, chain-of-thought), iteration as method, task types, good vs. bad prompts |
| **L3** | Context as Infrastructure | Persistent context, Claude Projects, Custom GPTs, M365 Copilot, system prompt design |
| **L4** | AI as Coworker | Delegation spectrum, Claude Cowork, ChatGPT Agent Mode, trust calibration, EU AI Act basics |

Plus a glossary with 27 key terms.

## How each lesson works

Every lesson follows a consistent pattern:

**OVERVIEW** (what and why) → **CORE CONCEPT** (the key idea) → **HOW IT WORKS** (step by step) → **EXAMPLES** (concrete, real-world) → **DO'S & DON'TS** (practical guardrails) → **TAKEAWAY** (one sentence you'll remember)

No code. No setup. Just clear explanations and actionable knowledge.

## Who is this for?

- **Knowledge workers** who use AI tools daily but never received structured training
- **Team leads and managers** who want their teams to use AI effectively and responsibly
- **Anyone curious** about AI beyond the hype — no technical background required
- **Prerequisites:** None. A browser and curiosity.
- **Time:** ~10-15 hours at your own pace

## FAQ

**Do I need technical skills?**
No. AI Fluency is designed for knowledge workers, not developers. Every lesson uses plain language and real-world examples. No coding, no terminal, no setup.

**How long does it take?**
About 10-15 hours total, at your own pace. Each lesson takes 20-40 minutes. You can stop and resume anytime.

**Can I skip levels?**
Yes, but each level builds on the previous one. If you're already comfortable with basic prompting, start at Level 3 (Context as Infrastructure).

**Is it free?**
Yes. Fully open source under the MIT license. No paywalls, no upsells, no email gates.

**Is it available in English?**
Yes. Every lesson is available in both English and German. Use the language switcher on the website.
